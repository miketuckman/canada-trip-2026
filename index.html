The Big Picture

One clean loop — pick your version of it

We fly into Halifax and out of Halifax, and everything in between chains together. The front-runner (Plan A) skips the international islands entirely: ferry from our friends' end of PEI to Québec's Îles de la Madeleine — every leg runs daily, so the dates bend to us. Plans B–D keep Newfoundland + Saint-Pierre (France), where Air Saint-Pierre's Sun/Wed and Mon/Wed/Fri schedules pin the dates. 🏨 Auberge Saint-Pierre — the island's 3-star
🏨 Nuits Saint-Pierre — 5-room boutique, book direct

🚨 Plan A's feasibility gate: the ferry slot & the room

Our window (Jul 20–24) sits inside Québec's construction holiday (Jul 19 – Aug 1) — the two weeks half the province vacations, and the Madeleines are their favorite beach. Two things must confirm before this plan is real:

⛴️ CTMA vehicle reservation, Souris ↔ Cap-aux-Meules, both directions (1-888-986-3278 / traversierctma.ca). Extra night sailings were added for summer 2026 — a hint of demand. Fallback: cross as foot passengers and rent a car or scooters on-island (limited inventory).

🛏️ Island lodging for 4 nights — Québécois families book by winter. Check tourismilesdelamadeleine.com listings + hotels (Château Madelinot, Auberge Madeli) same day as the ferry.

Silver lining: because the ferry runs daily, the window itself can slide — that's what the drag handles on the calendar are for. If lodging exists Jul 21–25 instead, we just drag.

🧭 Good to know: the Madeleines are the one corner of Québec on Atlantic time — no clock change from PEI. The crossing is ~5 hours each way; bring a book and watch for whales.

For the skeptics

The fine print

Every routing above survived a research pass — here's the reasoning, collapsed so it doesn't get in the way of the pretty pictures.

🏝️ Plan A: the Madeleine Islands math

The Îles de la Madeleine are reached from our loop exactly one way: the CTMA ferry from Souris, PEI — ~5 hours across the Gulf, daily in summer (roughly 1:00p out of Souris, 7:00a returns, plus extra night sailings added for 2026). There are no useful flights: Pascan serves the islands only from Québec City, Montréal, Bonaventure and Gaspé — nothing from Halifax. That makes Plan A the simplest plan logistically (one rental car end-to-end, two flights total, four ferry rides) and the cheapest on paper — and because every leg runs daily, it's the only plan whose dates can slide freely (hence the drag handles). Its risks are concentrated instead: vehicle deck space and island lodging during Québec's construction holiday (Jul 19–Aug 1), both of which book far ahead. One more check before committing: confirm the rental agency permits the car on the CTMA ferry. Bonus trivia: the Madeleines run on Atlantic time — the only bit of Québec that does — so no clock changes all trip.

✈️ Why the Orlando flight costs $432 and not $274

Google Flights' headline price for Wed Jul 15 is $274 for two — but that's Basic Economy: no carry-on, no seat selection, no changes. Checked bags cost $50/person in every tier, so the honest comparison is $374 (Basic + 2 bags) vs $482 (Economy + 2 bags). We're taking Economy + 1 checked bag = $432 for two: free carry-ons, we sit together, and — critical on a trip with more flights and ferries downstream — free changes. Jul 15 was the cheapest day of the whole week we sampled (every other day: $427–$511), and it's also the cheapest United day, which matters because we're burning a United travel credit.

🛩️ The Air Saint-Pierre schedule that shapes Plans B–D

Verified against the airline's own timetable (Jan 2026 update). St. John's ↔ Saint-Pierre runs only Sundays & Wednesdays (PJ1127/1128, 45 min: Sun 3:00p out / Wed 5:00p out; returns Sun 2:00p / Wed 4:00p). Halifax ↔ Saint-Pierre runs Mon/Wed/Fri plus summer Sundays (PJ1121/1122, ~1h15–1h30: outbound Mon 3:30p, Wed/Fri 1:00p, Sun 6:30p; returns Mon 11:00a, Wed/Fri 9:00a, Sun 5:00p). The Friday 9:00a Saint-Pierre→Halifax departure is the keystone of Plan B. Correction: an earlier draft of this plan had the St. John's days wrong — this is the airline's own table. No flights anywhere on July 14 (Bastille Day), which we dodge entirely by arriving the 15th. The Fortune, NL ferry (~1h30 crossing) exists as a capacity fallback but sits a 4-hour drive from St. John's.

⛴️ Why we fly to Newfoundland instead of taking the car ferry (Plans B–D)

Marine Atlantic's Port aux Basques ferry is 6–7 hours — followed by a 9–10 hour drive across the island to St. John's. The Argentia route is a 15-hour overnight sailing, seasonal, roughly 3x/week. Either burns 1–2 full days each way against a 2–4 day stay. The Halifax→St. John's flight is under 2 hours with several departures daily. Same logic kills Gros Morne in every plan: it's an 8-hour drive from St. John's, and while a St. John's→Deer Lake flight exists (~1h10, ~C$320), we'd need a second rental car in a province where rentals are the scarcest commodity going.

🚗 Rental cars & the Newfoundland car crunch

Plan A: one car, end to end — Halifax airport pickup, onto the Wood Islands ferry, onto the CTMA ferry (confirm the agency allows it), back to Halifax airport on departure day. Plans B–D: two short rentals — one for the Halifax days, returned before flying east; a second picked up the day we land back from Saint-Pierre (or St. John's, in Plan D), which rides the ferry to PEI. St. John's itself: Turo (peer-to-peer) — confirmed cars available. The traditional agencies in Newfoundland never rebuilt their fleets after COVID; July weeks have run $1,300–$2,900 when available at all. Turo gets booked the same day the Saint-Pierre hotel confirms.

🗓️ What happened to "back by July 25"?

The original target window was July 10–25. Plan A gets home July 26 — one day over. The Saint-Pierre plans run longer because Air Saint-Pierre's schedule quantizes the windows and the requested St. John's + Saint-Pierre days have to fit around them: Plan B lands July 29, Plan D July 30, Plan C August 1. Pulling any of them inside July 25 would gut either PEI or Saint-Pierre — the two legs with people and scarce rooms waiting. PEI is already trimmed to 5 nights across the board.
