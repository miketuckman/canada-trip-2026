🧭 Under consideration: Gander + Gros Morne National Park

Gander is ~3.5–4 hrs drive from St. John's. Gros Morne is another ~4.5–5 hrs past Gander (~7–8 hrs / 700km total from St. John's) — essentially the opposite coast of the island. That's not a manageable add to the current 2-day, Avalon-focused Newfoundland budget as a drive.

Better option: Deer Lake (YDF), the airport for Gros Morne, has direct flights to both Halifax and St. John's — fly in rather than drive across the island. Gander sits roughly at the midpoint, so it works well as a waypoint if we do decide to drive between St. John's and Gros Morne instead.

Realistically, adding both means +3–4 days on the Newfoundland leg — worth deciding whether that pushes the trip past July 25 or trims time elsewhere (PEI, Halifax).

Leg by Leg

✈️ Getting There open item

Jacksonville, Orlando, Daytona, and Sanford were all considered as gateway airports. We initially favored Orlando for a nonstop to Halifax — but that route doesn't run in July (it's a winter/spring-only seasonal service). In July, every candidate airport requires a connection, most likely through Toronto or Montreal.

Still need: a live fare/itinerary comparison across all four airports before booking.

🏙️ Halifax confirmed plan

1–2 days visiting Halifax friends. Rent a car here for local touring (e.g. Peggy's Cove), then return it before flying north — no reason to pay for it to sit at the airport while we're away.

✈️ Halifax → Newfoundland confirmed plan

Fly, don't drive-ferry. The Marine Atlantic car ferry runs 6–7 hours (Port aux Basques, then still a 9–10 hour drive across the island) or a seasonal 14–15 hour overnight to Argentia — either would burn a full day or more against just a ~2-day NL budget. The direct Halifax↔St. John's flight is under 2 hours.

✅ Newfoundland lodging & car — looking better than feared

Lodging: plenty of VRBO options found in both St. John's and Saint-Pierre — no longer a concern.

Rental car: Turo listings confirmed available in St. John's. The island-wide shortage is real (see below), so this is still worth booking early rather than waiting, but it's no longer a hard blocker.

⚠️ Background: why Newfoundland rental cars are tight

St. John's has had a severe, well-documented rental car shortage every summer since COVID — traditional agency fleets on the island never rebuilt. May/June/July vehicles have reportedly sold out months in advance; when available through the major brands, weekly rates have run $1,300–$2,900. Turo (peer-to-peer) has been the reliable workaround — book it as soon as dates are set rather than waiting.

✈️ Newfoundland → Saint-Pierre confirmed plan

Fly instead of the Fortune ferry — Fortune is a ~4 hour drive each way from St. John's, so the ferry route effectively costs a full extra day versus a 45-minute flight. Air Saint-Pierre flies St. John's↔Saint-Pierre on Mon / Wed / Fri, plus a seasonal Sunday add (late June–August) — 4 weekly options in our window.

⚠️ Bastille Day blackout — July 14

Air Saint-Pierre runs no flights on July 14 on any route (St. John's, Halifax, Montreal, Paris, Miquelon) — the island shuts down to celebrate. Whatever dates we land on, the Newfoundland → Saint-Pierre leg needs to be scheduled around this.

✈️ Saint-Pierre → Halifax confirmed plan

Air Saint-Pierre also flies direct Saint-Pierre↔Halifax (1h15m, same Mon/Wed/Fri/seasonal-Sun pattern) — this skips backtracking through St. John's on the way to PEI.

⛴️ Halifax → PEI confirmed plan

Skip the long drive around through New Brunswick to the Confederation Bridge — take the Wood Islands–Caribou ferry instead (Northumberland Ferries). About 1h15m, up to 8x/day in summer, and it carries the car. Pick up a fresh rental in Halifax on return from Saint-Pierre and drive it straight onto the ferry.

🏝️ PEI confirmed plan

5 days with our PEI friends, back on their home turf. Car comes with us via the ferry.

✈️ PEI → Home open item

We loop back to Halifax (rather than flying out of tiny Charlottetown) for the trip home — Halifax is a much larger airport with far more frequent Toronto/Montreal connections. Charlottetown only reaches Orlando via Toronto/Montreal or a Boston connection on JetBlue, with less frequency. Needs a live fare check closer to booking.
