### Halifax ‚Üî PEI
Skip the drive up through New Brunswick to the Confederation Bridge ‚Äî use the **Wood Islands (PEI) ‚Äì Caribou (NS) ferry** instead (Northumberland Ferries). ~1h15m crossing, up to 8x/day in summer, carries vehicles. ### Halifax ‚Üî Newfoundland (St. John's)
**Fly, don't drive-ferry.** The Marine Atlantic car ferry (North Sydney‚ÜîPort aux Basques, ~6-7h, then still a ~9-10h drive across the island to St. John's; or the seasonal North Sydney‚ÜîArgentia route, ~14-15h) would burn 1-2 full travel days each way against only a ~2-day NL budget. Direct flight Halifax (YHZ) ‚Üî St. John's (YYT) is ~1h55m on Air Canada/Porter/WestJet, multiple times daily.

### ‚úÖ Newfoundland rental car & lodging ‚Äî update, no longer blockers
Mike confirmed **Turo has cars available in St. John's** and found **plenty of VRBO listings in both St. John's and Saint-Pierre**. The underlying shortage is still real (see below) ‚Äî book early rather than wait ‚Äî but this is downgraded from a hard blocker to a "lock it in soon" item.

**Background ‚Äî why this was flagged:** St. John's/NL has had a severe, well-documented rental car shortage every summer since COVID (agency fleets on the island never rebuilt to pre-pandemic levels, unlike mainland Canada). Per CBC reporting, vehicles for May/June/July have reportedly sold out months in advance through the major brands (Avis/Budget/Enterprise/National/Hertz); when available, weekly rates have run **$1,300‚Äì$2,900**. A car is genuinely necessary to tour the Avalon Peninsula (Cape Spear, Witless Bay, the Irish Loop) ‚Äî there's no meaningful rideshare or transit alternative. Turo (peer-to-peer, active in St. John's since 2022) has been the reliable workaround.
