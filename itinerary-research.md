# Canada Trip 2026 — Halifax / PEI / Newfoundland / Saint-Pierre

Last updated: 2026-07-01

## Trip Parameters
- **Window:** July, likely mid-to-late July 2026 (targeting somewhere in the July 10–25 range)
- **Origin:** Jacksonville, FL area — Orlando/Daytona/Sanford also considered as gateway airports
- **Destinations & duration:** Halifax (1-2 days), PEI (5 days), Newfoundland/St. John's (~2 days), Saint-Pierre (~2 days) — roughly 10-11 destination days plus travel days, fits inside the window with flex room
- **Group:** PEI-based friends join for the Newfoundland/Saint-Pierre leg; Halifax-based friends visited at the start of the trip
- **Priority:** minimize wasted time over minimizing cost ("time over money")

## Recommended Route
Halifax (visit Halifax friends) → PEI friends travel up to Halifax → fly together to St. John's, NL → NL touring → fly to Saint-Pierre → fly back to Halifax → PEI (5 days, car ferry) → back to Halifax → fly home.

This ordering keeps both friend groups together for the NL/SPM leg and avoids backtracking (no leg is repeated).

### Halifax ↔ PEI
Skip the drive up through New Brunswick to the Confederation Bridge — use the **Wood Islands (PEI) – Caribou (NS) ferry** instead (Northumberland Ferries). ~1h15m crossing, up to 8x/day in summer, carries vehicles. Note: no 11:45/13:30 sailings on July 4, Aug 1, Sept 5, 2026 — not in your window but a sign the schedule has occasional exceptions worth double-checking near your actual dates.

### Halifax ↔ Newfoundland (St. John's)
**Fly, don't drive-ferry.** The Marine Atlantic car ferry (North Sydney↔Port aux Basques, ~6-7h, then still a ~9-10h drive across the island to St. John's; or the seasonal North Sydney↔Argentia route, ~14-15h) would burn 1-2 full travel days each way against only a ~2-day NL budget. Direct flight Halifax (YHZ) ↔ St. John's (YYT) is ~1h55m on Air Canada/Porter/WestJet, multiple times daily.

### ✅ Newfoundland rental car & lodging — update, no longer blockers
Mike confirmed **Turo has cars available in St. John's** and found **plenty of VRBO listings in both St. John's and Saint-Pierre**. The underlying shortage is still real (see below) — book early rather than wait — but this is downgraded from a hard blocker to a "lock it in soon" item.

**Background — why this was flagged:** St. John's/NL has had a severe, well-documented rental car shortage every summer since COVID (agency fleets on the island never rebuilt to pre-pandemic levels, unlike mainland Canada). Per CBC reporting, vehicles for May/June/July have reportedly sold out months in advance through the major brands (Avis/Budget/Enterprise/National/Hertz); when available, weekly rates have run **$1,300–$2,900**. A car is genuinely necessary to tour the Avalon Peninsula (Cape Spear, Witless Bay, the Irish Loop) — there's no meaningful rideshare or transit alternative. Turo (peer-to-peer, active in St. John's since 2022) has been the reliable workaround.

### 🧭 Newfoundland side trips — decisions (2026-07-01)
**Gander: IN, as a day trip from St. John's.** ~336 km / ~3.5 hrs each way on the Trans-Canada Highway (~7 hrs round trip). Draw is the transatlantic aviation history — North Atlantic Aviation Museum (#1 TripAdvisor attraction in Gander; Adults $10, ~30-45 min inside plus free outdoor aircraft), the "Come From Away" 9/11 story (includes WTC steel), and the Silent Witness memorial (Arrow Air crash). Done as a full-day loop from a St. John's base — no lodging relocation.

**Gros Morne National Park: OUT (leaning against, effectively dropped).** Far west coast of the island, ~700 km / ~7–8 hr drive from St. John's — not reachable by car within the NL budget. Geography for reference:
- St. John's → Gander: ~336 km, ~3.5 hr.
- St. John's → Gros Morne (Rocky Harbour): ~700 km, ~7–8 hr.
- Gander → Gros Morne (Rocky Harbour): ~334 km, ~4.5–5 hr.

Flying was researched at Mike's request: **St. John's (YYT) → Deer Lake (YDF)** — the airport serving Gros Morne — runs **~15x/week, ~1h10m, from ~C$320** (PAL Airlines / Provincial Airlines is the nonstop operator; Air Canada also bookable). Confirmed we'd only ever fly to Deer Lake from St. John's, not elsewhere. **The blocker isn't the flight — it's the car:** Deer Lake is just an airport, so touring Gros Morne needs a *separate* rental there, on top of NL's island-wide car-supply crunch, plus +3–4 days. Net decision: skip Gros Morne this trip.

### Newfoundland ↔ Saint-Pierre (SPM)
**Fly, don't take the Fortune ferry.** Fortune is roughly a 4-hour drive from St. John's each way — the ferry route (1.5h crossing, but only reachable via that long drive) effectively costs a full extra day versus a 45-minute flight. (The Fortune ferry remains the capacity fallback if ASP's small ATR-42s sell out.)

### ⚠️ Correction: Halifax–Orlando is NOT nonstop in July
Verified directly against Halifax Stanfield's own published route schedule: both Air Canada's and Porter's Halifax↔Orlando nonstop service are **winter/spring-only seasonal routes** (roughly late Oct 2026 – late March 2027). No nonstop Halifax–Orlando service exists in July.

Practical effect: the flight home in July requires a connection (most likely via Toronto or Montreal) regardless of whether the trip ends in Halifax or Charlottetown. Halifax is still probably the better ending point — it's a much larger airport with far more frequent Toronto/Montreal connections (multiple daily on Air Canada and WestJet) than Charlottetown, which only reaches Orlando via Toronto/Montreal or a JetBlue routing through Boston with less frequency. This needs a live fare/itinerary check closer to actual booking, but the underlying logic (bigger hub → shorter, more reliable connections) holds independent of the nonstop error.

### Car rental strategy ("double drop")
Rent in Halifax for the initial 1-2 days there, return it before flying to NL (no reason to pay for it sitting at the airport for ~4-5 days while you're in NL/SPM), then pick up a fresh rental in Halifax on return for the PEI leg (drive it onto the Wood Islands ferry), and drop off in Halifax at the very end. One-way Halifax→Charlottetown drop-off is available through Enterprise/National if needed, but isn't necessary since the loop ends back in Halifax anyway.
