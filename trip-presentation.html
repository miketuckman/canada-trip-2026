🚨 The one thing we must book NOW: a room

July lodging on Saint-Pierre is nearly gone. VRBO shows almost nothing (earlier optimism was wrong — corrected). The island has only a handful of hotels, and they're small:

🏨 Hôtel Robert — the big one (~40 rooms), waterfront, the Al Capone place
🏨 Auberge Saint-Pierre — the island's 3-star
🏨 Nuits Saint-Pierre — 5-room boutique, book direct
🛏️ Plus a short list of B&Bs on the official tourism site — several only take bookings by email.

The play: email/check all of them for the three date windows below, take whichever answers yes, and the rest of the trip snaps around it (that's literally what the plan switcher on this page is for). Summer dinner reservations are also near-mandatory — restaurants get booked the same week as the room.

For the skeptics

The fine print

Every routing above survived a research pass — here's the reasoning, collapsed so it doesn't get in the way of the pretty pictures.

✈️ Why the Orlando flight costs $432 and not $274

Google Flights' headline price for Wed Jul 15 is $274 for two — but that's Basic Economy: no carry-on, no seat selection, no changes. Checked bags cost $50/person in every tier, so the honest comparison is $374 (Basic + 2 bags) vs $482 (Economy + 2 bags). We're taking Economy + 1 checked bag = $432 for two: free carry-ons, we sit together, and — critical on a trip with five more flights — free changes. Jul 15 was the cheapest day of the whole week we sampled (every other day: $427–$511), and it's also the cheapest United day, which matters because we're burning a United travel credit.

🛩️ The Air Saint-Pierre schedule that shapes everything

Verified against the airline's own timetable (Jan 2026 update). St. John's ↔ Saint-Pierre runs only Sundays & Wednesdays (PJ1127/1128, 45 min: Sun 3:00p out / Wed 5:00p out; returns Sun 2:00p / Wed 4:00p). Halifax ↔ Saint-Pierre runs Mon/Wed/Fri plus summer Sundays (PJ1121/1122, ~1h15–1h30: outbound Mon 3:30p, Wed/Fri 1:00p, Sun 6:30p; returns Mon 11:00a, Wed/Fri 9:00a, Sun 5:00p). The Friday 9:00a Saint-Pierre→Halifax departure is the keystone of Plan A. Correction: an earlier draft of this plan had the St. John's days wrong — this is the airline's own table. No flights anywhere on July 14 (Bastille Day), which we now dodge entirely by arriving the 15th. The Fortune, NL ferry (~1h30 crossing) exists as a capacity fallback but sits a 4-hour drive from St. John's.

⛴️ Why we fly to Newfoundland instead of taking the car ferry

Marine Atlantic's Port aux Basques ferry is 6–7 hours — followed by a 9–10 hour drive across the island to St. John's. The Argentia route is a 15-hour overnight sailing, seasonal, roughly 3x/week. Either burns 1–2 full days each way against a 2–4 day stay. The Halifax→St. John's flight is under 2 hours with several departures daily. Same logic kills Gros Morne in every plan: it's an 8-hour drive from St. John's, and while a St. John's→Deer Lake flight exists (~1h10, ~C$320), we'd need a second rental car in a province where rentals are the scarcest commodity going.

🚗 The two-rental-car strategy & the Newfoundland car crunch

Rental #1: Halifax, 2 days, returned before we fly east — no car pays airport parking while we're in Newfoundland and France. Rental #2: picked up at Halifax airport the day we land from Saint-Pierre (or St. John's, in Plan C); it rides the Wood Islands ferry to PEI and comes back to the airport on departure day. St. John's: Turo (peer-to-peer) — confirmed cars available. The traditional agencies in Newfoundland never rebuilt their fleets after COVID; July weeks have run $1,300–$2,900 when available at all. Turo gets booked the same day the Saint-Pierre hotel confirms.

🗓️ What happened to "back by July 25"?

The original target window was July 10–25. Anchoring on the July 15 fare and adding the requested extra days in St. John's and Saint-Pierre pushes the return to July 30 (Plans A & C). Getting back inside July 25 would mean gutting either PEI or Saint-Pierre — the two legs with people and scarce rooms waiting. Recommendation: accept the 30th. Plan B pushes to Aug 1; if that's too far, its PEI leg trims from 4½ days to 4.
