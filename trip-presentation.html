The Big Picture

One clean loop — pick your version of it

We fly into Halifax and out of Halifax, and everything in between chains together. Newfoundland is out — the trip is now three shapes. Plan A skips the international islands entirely: ferry from our friends' end of PEI to Québec's Îles de la Madeleine (conditional on walk-on checks — the car decks sold out). Plans B and C are the same France trip on different flight windows, because aligning Air Saint-Pierre seats with a place to sleep has become the whole puzzle: B is the Sunday window (more France, no roof found yet), C is the Monday window with a bookable 3BR townhouse. There are no PEI↔Saint-Pierre flights, so Halifax is the hinge both ways. This page is interactive: switch plans and the calendar, map, and chapters all update — and on Plan A you can drag the calendar itself.

For the skeptics

The fine print

Every routing above survived a research pass — here's the reasoning, collapsed so it doesn't get in the way of the pretty pictures.

🏝️ Plan A: the Madeleine Islands math

The Îles de la Madeleine are reached from our loop exactly one way: the CTMA ferry from Souris, PEI — ~5 hours across the Gulf, daily in summer (roughly 1:00p out of Souris, 7:00a returns, plus extra night sailings added for 2026). There are no useful flights: Pascan serves the islands only from Québec City, Montréal, Bonaventure and Gaspé — nothing from Halifax. That makes Plan A the simplest plan logistically and the cheapest on paper — and because every leg runs daily, it's the only plan whose dates can slide freely (hence the drag handles). Update Jul 2: the concentrated risk fired — CTMA's car decks are sold out for the entire window (checked in the live booking engine), so the bring-the-car version is dead and the plan proceeds as walk-on passengers with an island rental from Agence de Location des Îles, or not at all. Passenger seats typically outlast the vehicle deck, but that plus the island car are now the plan's two go/no-go checks. Bonus trivia: the Madeleines run on Atlantic time — the only bit of Québec that does — so no clock changes all trip.

✈️ Why the Orlando flight costs $432 and not $274

Google Flights' headline price for Wed Jul 15 is $274 for two — but that's Basic Economy: no carry-on, no seat selection, no changes. Checked bags cost $50/person in every tier, so the honest comparison is $374 (Basic + 2 bags) vs $482 (Economy + 2 bags). We're taking Economy + 1 checked bag = $432 for two: free carry-ons, we sit together, and — critical on a trip with more flights and ferries downstream — free changes. Jul 15 was the cheapest day of the whole week we sampled (every other day: $427–$511), and it's also the cheapest United day, which matters because we're burning a United travel credit.

🛩️ The Air Saint-Pierre schedule that shapes Plans B–D

Verified against the airline's own timetable (Jan 2026 update). Halifax ↔ Saint-Pierre runs Mon/Wed/Fri plus summer Sundays (PJ1121/1122, ~1h15–1h30: outbound Mon 3:30p, Wed/Fri 1:00p, Sun 6:30p; returns Mon 11:00a, Wed/Fri 9:00a, Sun 5:00p). The Friday 9:00a Saint-Pierre→Halifax departure is the keystone of Plans B and C — landing 9:30a, early enough for a same-day ferry to PEI. There are no PEI↔Saint-Pierre flights at all, which is why the France plans route through Halifax both ways. Live availability (booking engine, Jul 2): Halifax outbounds have seats Sun Jul 19 · Mon Jul 20 · Wed Jul 22 · Fri Jul 24 (C$392.67/person base) — Fri Jul 17 sold out, which killed the original window; returns have seats every scheduled day Jul 19–27 (C$329.89/person base); quoted refundable round trip for all four travelers: C$3,102.64, flat across dates. No flights anywhere on July 14 (Bastille Day), which we dodge entirely by arriving the 15th.

🗺️ What happened to Newfoundland?

Earlier drafts included St. John's (puffins, Cape Spear, a Gander day trip, even Gros Morne). It's out entirely now: aligning Air Saint-Pierre's small planes with scarce Saint-Pierre rooms was already the trip's hardest puzzle, and adding a third island with its own flight schedule, its own rental-car crisis (July weeks ran $1,300–$2,900 when available at all), and its own lodging hunt multiplied every risk. Newfoundland deserves its own trip — this one is France + the friends' islands, done well.

🚗 The rental car choreography

Plan A: one car, end to end — Halifax airport pickup, onto the Wood Islands ferry, parked at the Souris terminal during the Madeleine days (car decks sold out), back to Halifax airport on departure day. Plans B–C: two short rentals — one for the Halifax days, returned before flying to France; a second picked up the Friday morning we land back, which rides the ferry to PEI and returns to the airport on the way home. No car needed on Saint-Pierre — the town is walkable and Île aux Marins/Miquelon go by boat.

🗓️ What happened to "back by July 25"?

The original target window was July 10–25. Plan A gets home July 26 — one day over. Plans B and C both land July 29: the Jul 17 flight sellout pushed B's France window to Jul 19–24 (its two freed days went to Halifax; a PEI warm-up variant lands Jul 27 but double-crosses the ferry). Pulling any plan inside July 25 would gut either PEI or Saint-Pierre — the two legs with people and scarce rooms waiting. PEI is already trimmed to 5 nights across the board.
