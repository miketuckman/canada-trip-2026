The Route Halifax Saint-Pierre Newfoundland Madeleines PEI
The Big Picture

One clean loop — pick your version of it

We fly into Halifax and out of Halifax, and everything in between chains together — four shapes to choose from. Plan A skips the international islands: ferry from our friends' end of PEI to Québec's Îles de la Madeleine (conditional on walk-on checks — the car decks sold out, and no rental cars have turned up on the island). Plans B and C are the same France trip on different flight windows, because aligning Air Saint-Pierre seats with a place to sleep has become the whole puzzle: B is the Sunday window (more France, no roof found yet), C is the Monday window with a booked 3BR townhouse. Plan D is the big one — Plan C's France, then instead of flying back we sail the Thursday ferry into Newfoundland, spend a few days around St. John's, and loop home through PEI. There are no PEI↔Saint-Pierre flights, so Halifax is the hinge. This page is interactive: switch plans and the calendar, map, and chapters all update — and on Plan A you can drag the calendar itself.

03

Newfoundland 🇨🇦

⛴️ The crossing that makes this plan — and the ground game after it

This is the leg that only exists because of one boat. SPM Ferries sails Saint-Pierre → Fortune, NL on Thursday Jul 23 at 10:00a (~1h30 crossing, verified on the 2026 calendar) — so we check out of the townhouse that morning and step straight onto it. No bridge night to solve; the ferry is the exit.

Fortune → St. John's is the one stretch to arrange. Fortune sits at the tip of the Burin Peninsula — 360 km / ~4¾ hrs from St. John's, and there are no car-rental counters at the wharf. The clean answer is a daily shuttle van up the peninsula: Foote's Taxi & Bus (Grand Bank, ~$70/pp · 1-800-866-1181 / 709-832-0491), or R&E Transportation (709-891-1866) / Da Boot Bussing (709-567-4703). Book a seat ahead — these are small vans.

🚗 Wheels in St. John's: pick up a rental at the airport (YYT) for the few days on the Avalon — cheap and plentiful compared to the islands. 🛏️ Lodging is easy here too (St. John's has real hotel inventory + plenty of Airbnbs, unlike Saint-Pierre).

🏁 Getting home: fly St. John's → Halifax (WestJet/Air Canada, ~1h30, several daily), collect the friends' car waiting at Halifax, and drive to the Wood Islands ferry for the PEI finish — then home out of Halifax.

🧭 Heads up: Newfoundland runs on its own clock — NL time is 30 minutes ahead of Atlantic, and Saint-Pierre (France) is 30 minutes behind Atlantic, so the short ferry hop crosses a full hour of time zone. The Burin drive is long but gorgeous; the shuttle lets everyone nap.
03

Saint-Pierre 🇫🇷

🏠 The lodging chase — and a real find

July lodging on Saint-Pierre is nearly gone, and aligning flights with rooms has become the whole puzzle. The find (Jul 2): an entire 3-bedroom townhouse — sleeps 5, 3 beds, 1.5 baths, 4.93★ across 30 reviews, Superhost, $840 for 3 nights with all fees included, free cancellation before Jul 19 — available Jul 20–23. That's what Plan C is built around — ✅ BOOKED & CONFIRMED Jul 2 (free cancellation until Jul 19 keeps it reversible).

⚠️ The alignment catch — Thursday Jul 23 night: checkout is the 23rd, the flight home is Friday the 24th at 9:00a, and no flight leaves Saint-Pierre back toward Halifax on a Thursday. The bridge-night ladder, best to worst: 1) ask the host about extending to the 24th · 2) one hotel night for the 23rd — a single night ×4 people is a far easier ask than the multi-night blocks (Robert / Auberge / Nuits below) · 3) re-date the refundable return to the Wed Jul 22 9:00a flight (seats verified) — France drops to 2 full days, the townhouse's 3rd night goes unused, and PEI gains two bonus days · 4) or stop fighting the Thursday and lean into it — the 10:00a SPM→Fortune ferry sails that very morning (verified on the 2026 calendar), turning checkout day into the start of a Newfoundland leg. That's Plan D.

Plan B (the Sunday window, Jul 19–24, 5 nights) still needs its roof — the island's small hotel bench:
🏨 Hôtel Robert — the big one (~40 rooms), waterfront, the Al Capone place
🏨 Auberge Saint-Pierre — the island's 3-star
🏨 Nuits Saint-Pierre — 5-room boutique, book direct
🛏️ Plus B&Bs on the official tourism site — several email-only.

The play: both flight windows are quoted refundable at the same price — so whichever roof confirms first picks Plan B or C. Summer dinner reservations are also near-mandatory — restaurants get booked the same week as the room.

03

Îles de la Madeleine ⚜️

⛔ Status change (Jul 2): car decks are SOLD OUT — walk-on or bust

Checked the CTMA booking engine directly: no vehicle space on the ferry for our entire time in Canada, in either direction. Bringing the rental car is dead. (Cabins are gone too — irrelevant, both crossings are daytime.) The plan survives only as walk-on passengers, which needs two confirmations:

1️⃣ Passenger seats on our two sailings — out Souris→Cap-aux-Meules Mon Jul 20 (~1:00p), back CaM→Souris Fri Jul 24 (7:00a) — foot-passenger space usually outlasts the car deck, but verify and book: 1-888-986-3278 / traversierctma.ca. Our Halifax rental parks at the Souris terminal for the four days.

2️⃣ Wheels on the island — Agence de Location des Îles is the island's rental operator (cars, scooters; offices in Cap-aux-Meules town and at the airport · 1-888-818-4537). Call today: our window sits inside Québec's construction holiday (Jul 19–Aug 1), the islands' peak of peak. Scooters (~$90/day) are the fallback if the cars are gone — workable for four adults in good weather, honest to say it's a downgrade.

🛏️ Plus the standing item: island lodging for 4 nights (tourismilesdelamadeleine.com · Château Madelinot · Auberge Madeli).

If either check fails, Plan B becomes the plan. The calendar's drag handles still matter — if passenger seats or lodging exist on slightly different dates, slide the window.

🧭 Good to know: the Madeleines are the one corner of Québec on Atlantic time — no clock change from PEI. The crossing is ~5 hours each way; bring a book and watch for whales.
